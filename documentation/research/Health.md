### Health
- The human visual systems relies on various sources of information to interpret 3D shapes and the relative positions of objects. Cues mainly rely on a single or both eyes. 
- In order to ensure maximum confort its important to mimic how we naturally see the world to reduce any form of fatigue such as eyestrain as well as neck and arm fatigue.

 ### What can developers do?
 - Place models and objects on non-awkward postions that would make sense. For example in the CPR training the model would be on the floor.
 - Refrain users from keeping their arms up for a long period of time
 - Taking a break function (Break pop up message every 30 mins)
 - According to Microsoft,  the optimal zone for hologram placement is between 1.25 to 5m. However if holograms need to be close to the user, it is recommened that content will need to be faded out at 40cm and place a rendering clipping plane at 30cm to avoid any nearer objects.
 - The hololens should be rendered at framerates higher than 60 FPS to provide optimal user experience
 - Avoid gaze angles more than 10 degrees above the horizon
 - Avoid gaze angles more than 60 degrees below the horizon
 - Avoid neck rotations more than 45 degrees off center
